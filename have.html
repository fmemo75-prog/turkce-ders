Turkish Grammar · A1–A2

Turkish "var" & "yok"

How to express having, not having, and possession across all tenses — with audio practice & exercises.

01 How it works

Theory
Unlike English, Turkish has no verb "to have." Instead, it uses var (exists / there is) and yok (doesn't exist / there isn't). Ownership is expressed with a possessive suffix on the noun, then var or yok follows.
[Noun + possessive suffix] + var / yok
Arabam var → My car exists → I have a car

✅ Present — Positive

  • Arabam var — I have a car
  • Param var — I have money
  • Evim var — I have a house
  • Vaktin var mı? — Do you have time?

❌ Present — Negative

  • Arabam yok — I don't have a car
  • Param yok — I have no money
  • Evim yok — I have no house
  • Vaktin yok mu? — Don't you have time?

🕐 Past Tense

  • Arabam vardı — I had a car
  • Param yoktu — I had no money
  • Evim vardı — I had a house
  • Vaktim yoktu — I had no time

🔮 Future Tense

  • Param olacak — I will have money
  • İşim olmayacak — I won't have work
  • Evim olacak — I will have a house
  • Vaktim olacak — I will have time
Quick ref: Present+var Present−yok Past+vardı Past−yoktu Future+olacak Future−olmayacak
