Definite Past Tense
Di'li Geçmiş Zaman

Events you personally witnessed or experienced — how to form it, how to use it, and how to practise.

01 What is the Definite Past?

Theory

The Di'li Geçmiş Zaman (also called the witnessed past or simple past) is used for actions you personally saw, heard, or experienced — things you know for certain because you were there. It is the most direct and common past tense in Turkish.

Compare it to the miş'li geçmiş (reported past), which is used for things you heard about second-hand. The di'li past says: "I know — I was there."

Formation formula

Remove the infinitive -mek / -mak to get the verb stem. Then add the past suffix -di / -dı / -du / -dü (or -ti / -tı / -tu / -tü after voiceless consonants) followed by the personal ending.

Verb stem + -di/dı/du/dü (or -ti/tı/tu/tü) + personal ending
gel + di + m → geldim (I came)

🎯 Vowel Harmony — which suffix?

  • e, i → -di  (geldim, içtim)
  • a, ı → -dı  (yaptım, aldım)
  • o, u → -du  (oldu, uyudum)
  • ö, ü → -dü  (gördüm, güldüm)

🔇 Voiceless consonant rule

  • If stem ends in ç f h k p s ş t
  • Use -ti / -tı / -tu / -tü
  • içti → içtim (I drank)
  • yaptı → yaptım (I did)

❌ Negative — değil or -me/-ma

  • Add -me / -ma after stem:
  • gelmedim — I didn't come
  • yapmadım — I didn't do
  • gitmedik — we didn't go

❓ Question — add mı/mi/mu/mü

  • Add question particle mı/mi:
  • geldin mi? — did you come?
  • yaptın mı? — did you do it?
  • gitti mi? — did he/she go?
Common time expressions used with Di'li past: dün (yesterday) · geçen hafta (last week) · geçen yıl (last year) · dün gece (last night) · bu sabah (this morning) · az önce (just now) · o zaman (at that time) · çocukken (when I was a child)
