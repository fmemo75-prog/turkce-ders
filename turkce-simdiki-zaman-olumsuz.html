01 How it works

Theory
In Turkish, the present continuous tense uses the suffix -yor. To make it negative, you insert -mi / -mı / -mu / -mü between the verb stem and -yor. The negative marker always uses the four-way vowel harmony based on the last vowel of the stem. Verb stem + mi/mı/mu/mü + yor + personal ending
e.g. gel → gelmiyor → he/she is not coming
Last vowel in stemNegative suffixExample
e, i-miyorgelmiyor — not coming

o, u-muyorokumuyor — not reading
ö, ü-müyorgörmüyor — not seeing

Watch out: If the verb stem ends in a vowel (e.g. ye-, de-), the final vowel is dropped before adding the suffix: ye → yi → yimiyor (not eating), de → di → demiyor (not saying).
